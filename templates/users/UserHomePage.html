Understanding Dementia

Dementia is a condition affecting memory, thinking, and behaviour. Early prediction can enable timely intervention and improved quality of life.

What is Dementia?

Dementia is not a single disease but a group of brain disorders that lead to a progressive decline in cognitive function, interfering with daily activities and independence. Over 55 million people worldwide are living with dementia — a number projected to reach 139 million by 2050.

Symptoms to Watch For

Common signs include persistent memory loss, confusion about time or place, difficulty communicating, personality and mood changes, impaired reasoning, and the loss of ability to perform daily tasks without assistance.

Causes & Types

Alzheimer's disease accounts for 60–70% of cases. Other forms include vascular dementia, Lewy body dementia (4.6%), Parkinson's Disease Dementia (9.7%), and frontotemporal degeneration. Genetic factors like the APOE ε4 allele significantly increase risk.

OASIS-2 Input Features

Our model uses: SES (Socioeconomic Status), MMSE (Mini-Mental State Examination score), CDR (Clinical Dementia Rating), eTIV (Estimated Total Intracranial Volume), nWBV (Normalised Whole Brain Volume), and ASF (Atlas Scaling Factor) — from MRI scans.
